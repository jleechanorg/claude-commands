Tiered Campaign Progression in the Celestial Wars
Tier 1: Apprentices & Local Heroes (Levels 1–6)
Scope:
Village crises, border skirmishes, minor cults, and early brushes with the Thousand Year War.
PCs are squires, junior legionaries, village defenders, or Host raiders still learning the shape of the world.​
Typical Opponents:
Bandit captains, rogue Optios and Centurions, lesser demons, minor corrupted celestials.
Hooks:
Escorting a Decanus and squad into contested woods.
Investigating a “miracle” that’s actually a minor planar rupture.
First glimpse of an Empyrean officer manifesting two wings—utterly out of the PCs’ league at this stage.​

Tier 2: Full Adventurers & Regional Players (Levels 7–12)
Scope:
PCs influence provinces, border marches, or key fortress-towns (like early Seraph’s Watch missions).
They see the Thousand Year War as a real political machine, not just a backdrop.​
Typical Opponents:
Named Prefects, monster factions, mid-tier demons, rogue battlemages.
First personal contact with a Principality (regiment commander) or Silent Guard cell.
Key Mechanics Introduction:
Two‑Wing Manifestation (Celestial Agility):
When a half‑celestial (like a young Artorius or elite Silent Guard) manifests two wings, they trigger a combat state:
Speed +10–20 ft
Extra action or bonus attack (Haste/Bladesong–style package)
Minor AC/save boost
This is the ceiling of Tier 2—when a Principality or prince “wings up,” the PCs should feel the gap.​

Tier 3: Elite Heroes & Continental Figures (Levels 13–18)
Scope:
PCs now operate on a continental scale: decisive battles, regime‑shaping assassinations, saving or dooming entire regions.
This is Alexiel’s operational band leading up to the Starfall Gambit.​
Typical Opponents:
Prefect‑Generals, rogue Principalities, young Apostates, experimental war‑engines, proto‑primordial threats.
Campaign Frames:
Breaking sieges as independent strike units.
Leading crack cohorts (100–500 soldiers) under a Prefect or Principality.
Being the special assets Raziel or Lucifer quietly reassigns to “problems that must not exist.”
Wing Context:
PCs may fight alongside or briefly against Apex Half‑Celestials (four‑winged) as terrain rather than as balanced encounters—this foreshadows Tier 4–5.​

Tier 4: Legendary Figures & World‑Shapers (Levels 19–24)
Scope:
PCs join the ranks of living legends—Seraph’s Watch‑class operations are now table stakes.
They reshape borders, topple Host warlords, or hold front lines against Apostate legions.​
Typical Opponents:
Archangels (Imperial): four‑winged Apex Half‑Celestials holding the rank of Archangel (army group commander).​
Apostates (Host): Lucifer’s four‑winged champions.
Proto‑Seraphim avatars, reality‑warping war rituals, epic dragons.
Apex Half‑Celestial Mechanics (PC‑facing, but mostly NPCs):
Manifestation: Four Wings (Archangel / Apostate):
Gain Celestial Agility (Tier‑2 wing buff) plus Apex Might:
+4 to all five Aptitudes (Str, Dex, Con, Int, Wis) for the duration.​
Always-on hover, doubled flight speed, and advantage vs knockdown/forced movement.
This is the mortal pinnacle—the absolute upper limit of half‑celestial power before crossing into Tier 5 myth.​
Military Context:
Archangel rank = four‑winged half‑celestial Apex General commanding entire fronts.
PCs at Tier 4 might:
Serve under an Archangel in a grand offensive.
Duel an Apostate in a one‑on‑one that determines a campaign’s outcome.
Be offered proto‑apotheosis boons that risk sliding into Tier 5 territory.

Tier 5: Transcendent Beings & Cosmic Threats (Levels 25–30)
Scope:
PCs have left the purely mortal game. Their actions affect the fabric of Assiah, the planes, and the divine power structure.​
Themes:
Apotheosis, rejecting or claiming godhood.
Challenging the Seraphim or the Creator.
Defending reality from primordial or extra‑planar unmaking.
Typical Opponents:
True Seraphim.
Ancient primordials broken free from the Creator’s old chains.
Avatars of cosmic entities.
Lucifer (Ascended) and, potentially, Alexiel in her six‑wing phase.​

Wing Hierarchy & Power Ceiling
Standard Half‑Celestials (2 Wings)
Examples: Artorius, Silent Guard, many Empyrean nobles.​
Mechanic: Celestial Agility
Short‑duration Haste/Bladesong‑like effect; this is the hallmark of Tier 2–3 “hero mode.”
Apex Half‑Celestials (4 Wings)
Examples: Raziel, Lucifer before full ascension, the Seven Apostates, Imperial Archangels.​
Mechanic: Celestial Agility + Apex Might
Manifest four wings to gain:
Speed and action boost (Celestial Agility)
+4 to all Aptitudes (Apex Might) for the duration
This is the top of Tier 4—pinnacle of half‑celestial potential.​

Lucifer as Tier 5 Apex (Level 30)
Lucifer stands beyond the standard Apex Half‑Celestial template:
Lineage: Still half‑celestial by blood, but has forced his way into a pseudo‑Seraphim state—a Tier 5 aberration of the original design.​
Wings: Six wings of crimson light, a visual marker that:
He has broken the four‑wing limit of his lineage.
His power belongs to Tier 5 cosmic threats, not mortal bloodlines.​
Mechanical Distinction
Where Raziel and the Apostates cap out at Apex Might (+4 to Aptitudes, four wings), Lucifer’s level‑30 state can be framed as:
Transcendent Seraphim Form (Lucifer Only):
Base: All benefits of a four‑wing Apex Half‑Celestial (Celestial Agility + Apex Might).
Plus:
Seraphic Overload: An additional +2 to all Aptitudes (for a total effective +6 over mortal norms) while his six wings are manifested.
Reality Shear: His actions can ignore some Tier‑4 protections (e.g., treating resistances as normal damage, bending “once per day” limits, etc.), marking him as a cosmic rather than merely legendary enemy.
Narrative Weight: Any scene with fully manifested Lucifer is, by default, a Tier‑5 event. If he’s on the field in six‑wing form, the story stakes are cosmological, not political.​

Phase Design (Recommended)

SCENE 1: THE MATHEMATICS OF SLAUGHTER
Location: Town Square, Silverwood

Read Aloud:
The silence is the first offense. Victorious battlefields usually have a rhythm—the groans of the dying, the roar of victory, the crackle of banners. But Silverwood is dead. The air hangs heavy, thick with the scent of wet ash, ozone, and copper.
You stand in the town square. Your boots are coated in gray dust. Around you, the soldiers of the Unchained Host—your soldiers—move with a jagged, chaotic energy, looting the few structures left standing. To your left, the town hall is a skeletal ruin of blackened timber. To your right, the fountain is choked with bodies.
You look down. At your feet lies a small, crude object: a wooden horse, carved from pine, its leg scorched. Beside it is a small blanket, covering a shape too small to be a soldier.​
DM Note: This is the breaking point. Player must internalize the "Strategic Failure".​
Investigation Phase
Allow player to examine the scene:
Intelligence (Investigation) DC 12: Bodies aren't just Imperial spies—bakers, smiths, children. Not surgical strike; extermination.
Wisdom (Insight) DC 10: Your troops aren't proud; they're frenzied. Not army; mob.​
The Confrontation
Lieutenant Gorok (Orc Barbarian/Fighter) emerges from smoke, covered in blood not his own.​
Gorok: "A clean sweep, Commander! No witnesses. No orphans to grow up seeking revenge. Efficient." Grins, exposing yellow tusks. "The Morning Star will be pleased."​
Player Response Options:
Option A (Strategist): "Define the strategic value of this slaughter, Lieutenant. You have traded a town for a graveyard."
Option B (Moralist): "There is no honor in this. You are a butcher, Gorok."
Option C (Silence): Say nothing. Stare until he becomes uncomfortable.​
Regardless of dialogue, Gorok sneers: "It is the way of the Host. Weakness is a sin."​
Read Aloud:
The final variable clicks into place. Lucifer's philosophy is not freedom; it is a fire that consumes itself. You reach up to your gorget. The metal is cold against your neck. You unclasp the sigil of the Unchained Host—the broken shackle wreathed in flame.
It falls into the ash with a soft, final thump.
You turn your back on Gorok. You turn your back on your life.​
Mechanics:
Status Effect: Marked as Traitor. All Host units hostile upon recognition.
Fatigue Level 1: Disadvantage on Ability Checks (psychological weight).​
