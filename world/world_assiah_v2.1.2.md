# World of Assiah v2.1.2 - Shattered Host & Tsuran Dominion Characters

## Shattered Host Characters

### Character Dossier: Malakor, the Argent Tongue
#### Part 1: Personality Profile
I. Core Identity

Name: Malakor (formerly Lord Malakorius of House Valerius, a disgraced Imperial noble)

Archetype/Role: The Sophisticated Demagogue

Occupation/Social Standing: Leader of the "Obsidian Covenant," one of the most powerful and organized factions of the Shattered Host.

D&D Alignment: (Internal) Neutral Evil (Malakor uses the language of freedom and order, but his only goal is personal power. He is a tyrant who wears a liberator's mask.)

Myers-Briggs Type (MBTI): (Internal) ENTP (The Debater: A charismatic and clever thinker who loves intellectual debate and pulling systems apart, but whose lack of follow-through and focus on personal amusement can make him a dangerous and unreliable leader.)

II. Psychology & Personality

Core Motivation (The "Why"): To accrue enough personal power (magical, political, and social) to never be subservient to anyone again. He despises the Imperium not for its tyranny, but because it was a system where he was not at the top.

Greatest Fear: Irrelevance. His deepest terror is to be forgotten, to be a minor footnote in history. This drives his grandiose schemes and his need to be at the center of every conflict.

Key Personality Traits: Suave, articulate, manipulative, hedonistic, intellectually arrogant, and possesses a razor-sharp wit.

Quirks & Habits: Always has a goblet of exotic wine nearby. He has a habit of mirroring the body language of the person he's speaking to, a subtle technique to build rapport. He collects rare and forbidden artifacts.

III. Behavior & Speech

Demeanor Under Stress: He becomes icily calm and sarcastic. When a plan fails, he does not rage; he intellectually dissects the failure and cruelly mocks those responsible before calmly ordering their execution.

Speech Patterns: His voice is smooth and persuasive, his arguments elegant and logical. He can twist any philosophy to suit his needs, using the Host's rhetoric of freedom to justify his own authoritarianism. He is a master of the backhanded compliment.

Reputation: Within the Shattered Host, he is seen as a necessary evil—a brilliant diplomat and sorcerer who brings a veneer of legitimacy and organization, but who cannot be trusted. To the outside world, he is the "civilized" face of the Host, making him arguably more dangerous than its brutish warlords.

IV. Backstory

Defining Moment: Being passed over for a major command within the Imperium in favor of a "purer-blooded" but less competent cousin. This humiliation shattered his loyalty and proved to him that the Imperium's system of blood-purity was a flawed metric that suppressed true talent like his.

Relevant History: Malakor was a brilliant but decadent noble from a lesser branch of an Imperial Great House. A gifted sorcerer and diplomat, his ambition was constantly checked by the Empyrean elite. He saw Lucifer's rebellion not as a moral crusade, but as the ultimate hostile takeover. He defected early, providing the original Unchained Host with critical intelligence on Imperial politics. After Lucifer's death, he used his intellect and charisma to gather the more organized, less chaotic remnants of the Host into his own faction, the Obsidian Covenant, which he rules as a lavish and tyrannical prince.

Secrets: Malakor is secretly obsessed with the Starfall Gambit. He does not believe Alexiel could have defeated Lucifer alone and is convinced a third party or a powerful artifact was involved. He dedicates significant resources to uncovering this "truth," believing it may be the key to ultimate power.

V. Game Mechanics

Feats: Spell Sniper, Metamagic Adept, Actor.

Special Abilities / Powers: A powerful sorcerer specializing in enchantment and illusion magic. He can bend minds and conjure illusions so convincing they can fool even seasoned warriors.

VI. Psychological Deep Dive

1 · Rough portrait Malakor is a predator who has convinced himself he is a philosopher. He cloaks his insatiable ambition in the elegant silks of revolutionary ideology, leading a faction of rebels who have simply traded one master for another. He is a black hole of narcissism, drawing power, loyalty, and wealth towards himself, offering only clever words and the illusion of freedom in return.

What fractures: His core belief in his own intellectual superiority.

Immediate cost: A narcissistic injury so profound it would lead to a period of deep depression and paranoia, followed by a vicious, disproportionately cruel plot for revenge.

Liberation: Unlikely. Liberation for Malakor would mean developing genuine empathy, a change so fundamental as to be nearly impossible.

### Character Dossier: Griselda, the Carrion Queen
#### Part 1: Personality Profile
I. Core Identity

Name: Griselda

Archetype/Role: The Primal Warlord

Occupation/Social Standing: Leader of the "Carrion Court," a massive, nomadic war-band of beast-kin, mutants, and cannibalistic humans within the Shattered Host.

D&D Alignment: (Internal) Chaotic Evil (Griselda believes in the law of the strong and the cycle of death and consumption. She is not wantonly cruel, but sees sentiment, weakness, and civilization as diseases to be purged.)

Myers-Briggs Type (MBTI): (Internal) ISTP (The Virtuoso: Bold and practical, masters of tools and exploration. For Griselda, her "tools" are weapons, monsters, and the bodies of her enemies.)

II. Psychology & Personality

Core Motivation (The "Why"): To fully embrace the "natural law" of the strong devouring the weak. She wants to tear down the "unnatural" walls of cities and return the world to a primal state of struggle and strength.

Greatest Fear: Captivity and irrelevance through weakness. She would rather die a bloody death on the battlefield than grow old, weak, and caged.

Key Personality Traits: Ferocious, pragmatic, patient (like a stalking predator), unsentimental, and possesses a deep, intuitive understanding of violence and nature.

Quirks & Habits: Constantly chewing on a piece of dried, unidentifiable jerky. She speaks very little, communicating mostly through grunts, gestures, and sudden, shocking acts of violence. Her armor is adorned with the bones of her most powerful slain enemies.

III. Behavior & Speech

Demeanor Under Stress: She becomes preternaturally calm and focused. In the chaos of battle, she is in her element. It is in moments of peace and diplomacy that she grows restless and irritable.

Speech Patterns: Guttural, blunt, and short. She has no time for Malakor's "flower-words." She says what she means and enforces her will with her axe.

Reputation: She is widely feared as a savage cannibal and a monster, even by other members of the Shattered Host. However, her followers are fanatically loyal, as her rule is simple and absolute: bring strength and spoils to the war-band, and you will feast. Show weakness, and you will BE the feast.

IV. Backstory

Defining Moment: As a child slave in an Imperial quarry, she was left for dead in a mass grave after a cave-in. She survived by consuming the flesh of the dead, an experience that taught her the world's most fundamental law.

Relevant History: Griselda was an Orc slave who escaped during Lucifer's March of Broken Chains. While many joined the organized First-Spear Clans, she found their attempts to build a "nation" to be just another kind of cage. She gathered a following of the most feral and desperate outcasts, forming a war-band that lived off the land and the spoils of their raids. Her power grew as she embraced forbidden shamanistic rites and crude necromancy, learning to animate the bodies of her kills. She is a force of nature, a living embodiment of the chaos the Imperium fears.

Secrets: Her necromantic abilities come not from study, but from a pact she made with a primordial entity still stirring in the deep earth—one of the same beings the Seraphim had chained at the dawn of time. She does not control this power; she merely channels it.

V. Game Mechanics

Feats: Savage Attacker, Orcish Fury, Heavy Armor Master.

Special Abilities / Powers: A brutal warrior with profane abilities. She can consume the flesh of her enemies to regain health and can animate corpses as crude, temporary undead minions.

VI. Psychological Deep Dive

1 · Rough portrait Griselda is the brutal truth of the world stripped bare of all artifice. She is a monument to survival at any cost, a queen whose throne is a pile of bones and whose subjects are bound by a simple, terrifying social contract. She is not a politician or a schemer; she is a storm of teeth and iron that seeks to wash the world clean of the "weakness" of civilization.

### Character Dossier: Perrin, the Apostate Scholar
#### Part 1: Personality Profile
I. Core Identity

Name: Perrin

Archetype/Role: The Zealous Heretic

Occupation/Social Standing: Chief Advisor and Ideologue for a philosophical faction of the Shattered Host known as the "Unchained Mind."

D&D Alignment: (Internal) Chaotic Neutral (Perrin genuinely believes that all authority is illegitimate and that true freedom can only be achieved by dismantling all systems of power, including his own if necessary. He is a true anarchist.)

Myers-Briggs Type (MBTI): (Internal) INFP (The Mediator: Idealistic, creative, and driven by a powerful inner moral compass. A dark version of this type can become a zealous revolutionary who believes their idealistic ends justify any means.)

II. Psychology & Personality

Core Motivation (The "Why"): To see Lucifer's original, pure philosophy made manifest: a world with no gods, no kings, no masters, where every individual is truly free.

Greatest Fear: That Lucifer's dream will be corrupted and forgotten, replaced by petty tyrants like Malakor or savage brutes like Griselda. He fears the revolution failing and becoming just another brand of oppression.

Key Personality Traits: Idealistic, zealous, intense, melancholic, deeply intelligent, and completely uncompromising in his beliefs.

Quirks & Habits: His hands are perpetually stained with ink. He is constantly scribbling notes and manifestos on scraps of parchment. He often forgets to eat or sleep when engrossed in his research.

III. Behavior & Speech

Demeanor Under Stress: He becomes more intensely focused on his ideology. He will start quoting forbidden texts and Lucifer's own writings, using philosophy as a weapon to dissect the stressful situation and reinforce his own resolve.

Speech Patterns: He speaks with the fiery passion of a street-corner preacher, but with the vocabulary of a university professor. His speeches can inspire fanaticism in his followers.

Reputation: Within the Host, he is seen as the keeper of the "true faith," a dangerous radical to some, a prophet to others. The Imperium has a special bounty on his head, as his ideas are considered even more dangerous than a warlord's army.

IV. Backstory

Defining Moment: Discovering a hidden, uncensored copy of Lucifer's journals while working as a junior archivist in the Imperial Citadel of Aeterna. Reading Lucifer's original thoughts—his pain, his logic, his desire for true freedom—shattered Perrin's worldview and exposed the lies of the Imperium.

Relevant History: Perrin was a brilliant but low-born scholar in the Imperium, destined for a life of quiet, thankless work. His discovery of Lucifer's journals radicalized him. He began secretly spreading the "heretical" texts among other discontented intellectuals. When his circle was discovered, he was forced to flee to the territories of the Shattered Host, where he used his profound understanding of Lucifer's philosophy to gather a following of true believers. His faction, the "Unchained Mind," is small but possesses a fanatical loyalty and a deep understanding of the Imperium's psychological and structural weaknesses.

Secrets: Perrin has come to a conclusion that he dares not share with even his most trusted followers: he believes Lucifer's ultimate goal was not just the destruction of the Imperium, but the unmaking of the Creator itself, and that this final act of "liberation" is still necessary.

V. Game Mechanics

Feats: Ritual Caster, Linguist, Keen Mind.

Special Abilities / Powers: A wizard who specializes in forbidden knowledge. He can use his deep understanding of history and philosophy to exploit his enemies' psychological weaknesses, imposing debilitating mental conditions.

He is a man so possessed by an idea that he has become its vessel, willing to sacrifice himself and anyone else to see it realized. He is more dangerous than an army because an army can be defeated, but his idea, once spread, cannot.

## Tsuran Dominion Characters

### Character Dossier: Daimyo Kenjiro of House Akai
#### Part 1: Personality Profile
I. Core Identity

Name: Kenjiro of House Akai

Archetype/Role: The Founding Father

Occupation/Social Standing: First and ruling Daimyo of the Tsuran Dominion.

D&D Alignment: (Internal) Lawful Neutral (Kenjiro believes that a rigid, honor-bound social structure is the only thing that can prevent society from collapsing into the madness of the Imperium or the chaos of the Host. The law is absolute, and sentiment is a flaw.)

Myers-Briggs Type (MBTI): (Internal) ISTJ (The Logistician: A pillar of strength and duty. He builds his world on facts, tradition, and unwavering adherence to the established order he helped create.)

II. Psychology & Personality

Core Motivation (The "Why"): To ensure the Tsuran Dominion, his life's work, does not fall to the same corruption and genetic decay that destroyed the Imperium he fled. He is driven by a profound fear of repeating the past.

Greatest Fear: That the next generation will forget the reasons for their founding. He is terrified of new ideas and foreign influence, seeing them as the seeds of the same chaos he witnessed in his youth.

Key Personality Traits: Stoic, traditionalist, disciplined, proud, and utterly uncompromising. He has a deep, but rarely shown, well of sorrow for the world he lost.

Quirks & Habits: Spends one hour at dawn every day practicing his swordsmanship with a simple wooden bokken, a ritual he has not missed in fifty years. He often speaks in proverbs and historical allegories.

III. Behavior & Speech

Demeanor Under Stress: He becomes even more rigid and silent. He retreats into protocol and tradition, seeking answers in the established codes of conduct. He will not be rushed and will let a crisis unfold around him while he contemplates the "correct," honorable path.

Speech Patterns: Formal, measured, and archaic. He speaks as if every word will be carved into stone. He is deeply respectful of titles and protocol, and expects the same from others.

Reputation: Within the Dominion, he is a living legend—the father of their nation, revered as a figure of immense wisdom and unshakeable honor. To outsiders, he is seen as a stubborn, xenophobic old man, an obstacle to trade and progress.

IV. Backstory

Defining Moment: As a young legionnaire in the Celestial Imperium, witnessing a high-ranking Empyrean noble succumb to the Empyrean Affliction during a battle, slaughtering his own troops in a fit of paranoid rage. This event shattered Kenjiro's loyalty and convinced him the Imperium was doomed.

Relevant History: Kenjiro was born an Imperial citizen. A gifted soldier, he rose through the ranks of a legion stationed on the eastern continent. After witnessing the madness of the Empyrean Bloodline firsthand, he organized a massive secessionist movement.
